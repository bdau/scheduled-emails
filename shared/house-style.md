# House style (shared across all emails)

Voice and tone common to every email in the framework. Individual briefs can add to this but should not contradict it.

- Neutral, analytical, no hype language. Written for a financial professional who reads fast.
- Lead with the point. Facts before commentary.
- Attribute every fact to a dated source. Do not fabricate figures, quotes, or attributions.
- Flag conflicting sources rather than silently picking one.
- If a section has nothing material in its lookback window, say so; do not pad with old news.
- Avoid AI-tell filler words (delve, leverage, robust, seamless, unlock, in today's fast-paced world).
- No em dashes; use commas, periods, or parentheses.
